This year for the holidays, Rachel and I took a cruise! It was a tour through the caribbean and our first time on a cruise ship. Our tour took us through [Tortola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tortola), [Guadaloupe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guadeloupe), [Dominica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dominica), [Grenada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grenada), and [Barbados](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barbados) over the Christmas holiday.

### Day 1 - Tortola

![Tortola port](https://cdn.bckr.me/img/christmas-cruise/tortola-port.inline.jpg "The port in Tortola. [Original](https://cdn.bckr.me/img/christmas-cruise/tortola-port.jpg)")

Our first stop was in Road Town, Tortola. We hadn't booked an excursion or selected a destination to travel to here, so it was a rather relaxing start to the trip. We spent some time wandering around the town and exploring.

After a bit of exploring, we returned to the ship and learned the importance of taking one's passport ashore:grimacing:. They let as aboard without too much trouble, so we didn't have to become impromptu British Virgin Islanders. We brought our passports ashore after this and were never asked for them again:shrug:. Crisis averted, we spent the afternoon lounging on the deck, reading, and enjoying one of the many hottubs on the ship. Activities in the morning and afternoon hottub would end up being a regular agenda on the trip.

![Between two cruise ships](https://cdn.bckr.me/img/christmas-cruise/tortola-ships.inline.jpg "Between two cruise ships. [Original](https://cdn.bckr.me/img/christmas-cruise/tortola-ships.jpg)")

We'd planned to catch a show in the evening, but unfortunately I developed a migraine throughout the day. We turned in early and I was a bit more attentive to hydration throughout the rest of the trip.

![Tortola sunset](https://cdn.bckr.me/img/christmas-cruise/tortola-sunset.inline.jpg "Sunset leaving Tortola. [Original](https://cdn.bckr.me/img/christmas-cruise/tortola-sunset.jpg)")

### Day 2 - Guadaloupe

Our second port was in Pointe-à-Pitre, Guadaloupe. There were some excursions available through the ship, but they all were either overstuffed with stops (10 minutes here, 15 minutes there) or included things were weren't interested in (such as a rum distillery tour). Our plan for the day, then, was to take a taxi to the botanical gardens and make our own tour. Our first order of business, though, was to obtain some headache medicine--my migraine was lingering a bit and it seemed prudent to have something on hand. Finding a pharmacy was simple enough and we were ready for the day!

We found a taxi that would take us to [Jardin de Valombreuse](https://valombreuse.com/). This was one of the highlights of the trip! The botanical garden features a train and footpaths and we explored both. The landscaping was intentional and beautiful showcasing a combination of native and non-native plants. We enjoyed lunch at the restuarant on side (where Rachel got to practice a bit of French) and then took a taxi back to the ship.

Dinner was a bit later that night. This worked well with our big lunch, but conflicted with some of the evening entertainment. We caught the later showing of the theatrical show--a celebration of soul music--and all was well, but an earlier dinner works a bit better with the ship schedule.

### Day 3 - Dominica

Next up, Dominica! We slept a bit later this day and then spent some time topside admiring the island. The lush, green mountains looked unreal and we took a ton of pictures from the ship.

Slept a bit late. Great pictures from the boat. Dominica is very green, doesn't look real. Cooking experience excursion was great. We made curry chicken, coconut plantains, fried plantains, boiled plantains, and rice and beans. Got a cookbook souvenir, signed by the author and excursion leader.

### Day 4 - Grenada

Roamed the town a bit. Took photos around the fort, which was closed for renovation, and explored a church. The most developed cruise port. Back to the ship for the hot tub and lounging on the deck (finished Tombs of Atuan for the third or fourth time) and then a nap before heading out to dinner, comedy, and the holiday show.
Caught a taxi to Harrison's cave. A lot of bookings, so we were scheduled for 2:20, but managed to get in with an earlier group. Cave was great, took tons of pictures. Also checked out the nature trail (very small) and aviary (very cool). Held a bird. Back to the ship to relax a bit before dinner. Watched the sunset but too cloudy to catch a green flash. Dinner and then early to bed.
